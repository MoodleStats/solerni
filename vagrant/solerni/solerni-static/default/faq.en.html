FAQ

What is a collaborative MOOC?

A "MOOC" is an online learning pathway through which various participants interact and share their skills.

"Massive" means that this course can involve an unlimited number of participants.

"Open" shows that it is open to all, wherever they are located and regardless of their previous knowledge.

"Online" shows that it takes place online.

"Course" indicates that it has a start, a finish and a schedule.

MOOCs on our Solerni platform are collaborative as we champion social learning, that is "peer learning", rather than direct unilateral learning.

In addition to traditional courses which can be read or viewed via video, our MOOCs propose:

  • community functions which encourage interaction (forum),

  • content creation tools which encourage participant production (wiki, blog, resource sharing spaces...),

  • material which encourages activity and learner independence.

I want to join a MOOC

  • Who can join a MOOC?

Anyone can join a MOOC: MOOCs are open to everybody! You just need to register.

  • What equipment do I need?

To join a MOOC you will need a computer or a tablet and Internet access.

  • How can I sign up to a course?

Register with the Solerni platform to create an account. Registration is free: it gives you the right to access and follow all of the MOOCs offered by Solerni.

To register you just need to use your e-mail address or your Facebook account, and then click on the "Register" button to join the MOOC.

  • Do I have to pay for the courses?

MOOCs on the Solerni platform are free.

  • Is there a deadline for registering for a MOOC?

You can register at any time, but based on the schedule you may not be able to validate exams or work that you should have already submitted.

  • Is there an attestation at the end of the course?

Each MOOC can propose a particular certification or not.

To find out more, read the description for your course.

 

How does this MOOC take place?

  • How long does a MOOC last?

The duration is specific to each MOOC. To find out more, read the description for your course.

  • Are there any prerequisites?

Every course is different. Some have prerequisites, others not. Check the specific prerequisites for your course on the description page. If you do not meet all of them, you can still follow the course.

  • How much time should I dedicate each week?

The weekly working time depends on each MOOC. Generally it is between 2 and 5 hours each week. To find out more, read the description for your course.

  • Are there timetables?

There are no course timetables! You are free to log in to your course at any time, as the videos, online course and exercises are always available.

  • How are the courses organised?

The courses are divided into sequences.

Each week you will start a new sequence which is made up of subjects and activities, during which you will have the opportunity:

- to learn, through courses provided by teachers

- to communicate on the forums, with other learners and with teachers

- to share resources that you have created for the MOOC activities

  • How can I see my progress?

You progress is displayed on each course screen.

You will see your progress bar advance throughout as you complete the tasks set by the teachers. So that your progress bar advances, don't forget to click on the "Completed" button at the bottom of each course screen before passing to the next subject or activity.

By clicking on the "Completed" button, you are confirming that you have completed the requested task (reading, viewing, activity, quiz...)

  • I need help during the course

Are you have difficulty understanding a course or completing a task? Use the learner and teacher network to help you by discussing it directly on the forum, because "Learning, it's always better together"!

 

What is peer evaluation?

Skill badges are awarded by the community. This is what we call peer evaluation. If you wish to validate a skill, you must present several pieces of work in an "evidence file", which will be evaluated by three people who wish to validate the same skill. Evaluation is in accordance with precise criteria. Every person who wants to validate a skill agrees to peer evaluation.

I have a problem

  • I cannot log in

Are your password or e-mail address not recognised?

Click on Forgotten Password to reset your password and log in again.

Please check that your browser settings ensure that you have the most recent version of your browser, then try to log in again.
Please note that Solerni works best with Firefox.

If you cannot gain access, create a new account by using a different e-mail address.

  • I have not received a Solerni e-mail

Check that the e-mails have not gone into your "Spam" folder. If this is the case, add the "noreply@solerni.com" e-mail address to trusted e-mails.

  • I have display problems

Check your browser settings. We recommend that you download the most recent version of your browser.
Please note that Solerni works best with Firefox.
